Most Common Types of a Bass Guitar

Below are the 5 most common bass guitar form factors!

Precision Bass (P-Bass)

The P-Bass is famous for its split-coil pickup and chunky neck, it produces the deep, thumping low-end that defines the sound of rock and Motown.

Jazz Bass (J-Bass)

With two single-coil pickups and a slimmer neck, the J-Bass offers a brighter, growlier tone with more versatility and definition than the P Bass.

PJ Bass

The PJ Bass is a hybrid instrument that combines the warm thump of a P-Bass middle pickup with the biting attack of a J-Bass bridge pickup.

StingRay Bass

The StingRay Bass is known for its active electronics and large humbucker. It delivers a high-output, aggressive punch that is iconic for funk and slap bass.

Double (Upright) Bass

This massive, fretless acoustic instrument is played vertically to create the warm, woody, and percussive foundation of jazz and classical music.
